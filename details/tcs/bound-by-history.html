Bound by History

$1500

"Bound by History" is a poignant sculpture that confronts the viewer with the haunting echoes of a painful past. Crafted from brown wood that replicates the flooring of a slave ship, the piece immediately evokes the inhumane conditions endured by countless souls during the transatlantic slave trade. At its heart, the sculpture features two outreached hands, one on the left and the other on the right. These hands, cast and painted in gold, embody the collective yearning for connection and liberation. They reach toward each other, fingers stretching out in a desperate attempt to bridge the chasm created by a malevolent force.

Brown natural fiber rope forms a taut loop that runs between the two hands, symbolizing the unbreakable bond of shared suffering. This rope is more than a mere connection; it represents the collective spirit and resilience of those who endured unimaginable hardships.

Bronze chains, looped around each hand, are a stark reminder of the brutal force used to subjugate and control. The chains that connect the hands remain as symbols of the oppression endured, while the leftover chain that drizzles onto the "deck" serves as a testament to the enduring legacy of this dark period in history. "Bound by History" challenges viewers to confront the atrocities of the past, recognizing that the legacy of slavery still reverberates in the present. This sculpture is a powerful call for empathy, understanding, and an unwavering commitment to justice. Through this piece, we are reminded of the resilience and strength that enabled people to reach for connection and unity even in the most dehumanizing of circumstances.
